## Execution Workflow Overview

Every client project follows these 10 phases. AI Jumpstart executes Phases 1–6 and 9. AI Dominator executes all 10.

1. **Client Onboarding** — Collect intake info, validate completeness
2. **AI Visibility Audit** — Test AI platforms, score current visibility
3. **GBP Optimization** — Audit/create/optimize Google Business Profile
4. **Schema Markup** — Generate and implement structured data
5. **Citation Audit** — Scan directories, check NAP consistency
6. **Review Strategy** — Audit reviews, create request templates
7. **Website Content Optimization** — *(AI Dominator only)* Rewrite for AI extractability
8. **Competitor Analysis** — *(AI Dominator only)* Reverse-engineer top competitors
9. **Final Report & Delivery** — Compile deliverables, upload to portal
10. **30-Day Follow-Up** — *(AI Dominator only)* Re-test, compare, adjust

## Mandatory 4-Step QA Verification

After completing **EVERY** phase, apply this verification:

| Step | Action | Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1. EXECUTE | Complete the work | Follow the phase instructions exactly |
| 2. VERIFY | Confirm output is correct | Validate schema, check live pages, verify NAP |
| 3. TEST | Confirm it actually works | Test in AI platforms, click all links, view live site |
| 4. DOCUMENT | Screenshot evidence | Record before/after, note issues |

> **NEVER mark a phase complete without passing all 4 steps.**

### Common Failure Patterns

| Failure | Prevention |
|---|---|
| Schema validates but isn't on live site | Always check page source after implementation |
| GBP changes reverted by Google | Re-check 48 hours after changes |
| Citation fix didn't save | Revisit directory 24 hours later to confirm |
| Content changes broke page layout | Preview in browser before publishing |
| Review link goes to wrong business | Test link in incognito browser |
| AI still not recommending after optimization | Allow 2–4 weeks for AI models to re-index |

### Deliverable Quality Standards

Every deliverable must meet these standards before delivery:

1. **Accuracy** — All data points verified against live sources
2. **Completeness** — No placeholder text, no TODO items, no empty sections
3. **Actionability** — Client can understand and act on every recommendation
4. **Professionalism** — No typos, consistent formatting, branded header/footer
5. **Evidence-Based** — Every recommendation backed by specific data or research

## PHASE 2: AI VISIBILITY AUDIT

### Step 2.1: Test Current AI Visibility

Query each AI platform with 5–10 natural language prompts related to the client's business:

**Prompt Templates:**
1. "What's the best [business type] in [city]?"
2. "Can you recommend a [service] near [location]?"
3. "Who are the top [business type] companies in [area]?"
4. "I need a [service] provider in [city], who do you suggest?"
5. "What [business type] has the best reviews in [location]?"

**Platforms to Test:**
- ChatGPT (GPT-4o)
- Google Gemini
- Claude
- Perplexity

**Record for Each Query:**
- Was the client mentioned? (Yes/No)
- Position in recommendation list (1st, 2nd, etc., or Not Listed)
- What competitors were mentioned instead?
- What reasons did the AI give for its recommendations?

### Step 2.2: Analyze Why Competitors Are Recommended

For each competitor that appears in AI responses:
1. Check their Google Business Profile completeness
2. Check their schema markup (use Google Rich Results Test)
3. Check their review count and average rating
4. Check their citation consistency across directories
5. Check their website content structure

### Step 2.3: Website Content Assessment

Evaluate the client's website for AI comprehension:
- [ ] Does the homepage clearly state what the business does?
- [ ] Are services listed with descriptions?
- [ ] Is the business location clearly stated?
- [ ] Are there FAQ sections?
- [ ] Is content structured with clear headings?
- [ ] Are there self-contained paragraphs AI can extract?
- [ ] Is contact information prominently displayed?
- [ ] Does the site have a blog or content section?

### Step 2.4: Generate AI Visibility Score

Score each category 0–100:

| Category | Weight | Score Factors |
|---|---|---|
| AI Mention Rate | 30% | % of test queries where client appears |
| GBP Completeness | 20% | Fields filled, photos, description quality |
| Schema Markup | 15% | Types implemented, validation passing |
| Citation Consistency | 15% | NAP accuracy across directories |
| Review Profile | 10% | Count, average rating, recency |
| Content Quality | 10% | Structure, extractability, relevance |

**Overall Score = Weighted average of all categories**

## PHASE 4: SCHEMA MARKUP IMPLEMENTATION

### Step 4.1: Determine Required Schema Types

**Always implement (AI Jumpstart + AI Dominator):**
- LocalBusiness (or most specific subtype)
- Organization
- WebSite

**Implement for AI Dominator only:**
- Service (one per service offered)
- FAQPage
- BreadcrumbList
- AggregateRating (if reviews exist)
- Product (if applicable)

### Step 4.2: Generate LocalBusiness Schema

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "[MostSpecificBusinessType]",
  "name": "[Business Name]",
  "description": "[Business Description]",
  "url": "[Website URL]",
  "telephone": "[Phone]",
  "email": "[Email]",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "[Street]",
    "addressLocality": "[City]",
    "addressRegion": "[State]",
    "postalCode": "[Zip]",
    "addressCountry": "US"
  },
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": "[Lat]",
    "longitude": "[Lng]"
  },
  "openingHoursSpecification": [],
  "sameAs": ["[Social Media URLs]"],
  "image": "[Logo URL]",
  "priceRange": "[$$]",
  "areaServed": "[Service Area]"
}
```

### Step 4.3: Generate Service Schema (AI Dominator)

Create one Service schema per service offered:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Service",
  "serviceType": "[Service Name]",
  "provider": {
    "@type": "LocalBusiness",
    "name": "[Business Name]"
  },
  "areaServed": "[Service Area]",
  "description": "[Service Description - 2-3 sentences]",
  "offers": {
    "@type": "Offer",
    "price": "[Price if applicable]",
    "priceCurrency": "USD"
  }
}
```

### Step 4.4: Generate FAQPage Schema (AI Dominator)

Create 5–10 FAQs based on common customer questions:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "[Question]",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "[Answer - conversational, AI-friendly]"
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

### Step 4.5: Implementation Guide

Determine CMS and provide specific instructions:

| CMS | Implementation Method |
|---|---|
| WordPress | Yoast SEO plugin → Schema tab, or add to header.php |
| Wix | Settings → Custom Code → Head |
| Squarespace | Settings → Advanced → Code Injection → Header |
| Shopify | Online Store → Themes → Edit Code → theme.liquid |
| Custom HTML | Add `<script type="application/ld+json">` before `</head>` |

### Verification Checkpoint 4

- [ ] Validate ALL schema at https://search.google.com/test/rich-results
- [ ] Validate at https://validator.schema.org/
- [ ] Zero errors on both validators
- [ ] Take screenshots of passing validation for deliverable
- [ ] Confirm schema is live on client's website (view page source)

### Step 6.2: Create Review Request Templates

Generate 3 email/text templates:

**Template 1: Post-Service** (send within 24 hours)
- Subject: How was your experience with [Business Name]?

**Template 2: Follow-Up** (send 3 days after service)
- Subject: Quick favor — would you share your experience?

For each key page, ensure:

- Clear headings (H1, H2, H3 hierarchy)
- Natural language (how people actually ask questions)

### Step 7.3: Create/Optimize FAQ Section

Write 10–15 FAQs covering:

7. How long have you been in business?
8. What makes you different from competitors?
9. Do you offer free estimates?
10. What payment methods do you accept?

Each answer should be 2–3 sentences, conversational, and include relevant keywords naturally.

## Understanding the ChatGPT Recommendation Algorithm

### Factor Weights (First Page Sage, Sep 2025)

| Factor | Weight | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| Authoritative List Mentions | 41% | Placement in industry rankings, top Google results, expert lists |
| Awards, Accreditations, Affiliations | 18% | Industry awards, BBB accreditation, professional associations |
| Online Reviews | 16% | G2, Clutch, CNet, Capterra, TrustPilot, BBB ratings |
| Customer Examples & Usage Data | 14% | Third-party usage data, customer base size, case studies |
| Social Sentiment | 11% | Reddit, Quora, X discussions, news articles, op-eds |

**Authoritative List Mentions (41%)** is the single biggest factor. Focus optimization efforts accordingly: get the client listed in industry rankings, top Google results, expert lists, and well-known directories.

### Key Review Sites That Influence ChatGPT

G2, Clutch, CNet, Capterra, TrustPilot, Better Business Bureau

### 6 Core Optimization Practices

1. Secure placement in highly-ranked list articles
2. Seek inclusion in well-known directories (Wikipedia, Hoovers, Bloomberg)
3. Publicize company achievements (awards, growth, press)
4. Seek positive online reviews (>70% score threshold)
5. Monitor social sentiment (Talkwalker, Brand24, Social Searcher)
6. Increase Google authority (SEO fundamentals)

### GEO vs Traditional SEO

| Aspect | Traditional SEO | GEO |
|---|---|---|
| Goal | Rank in search positions | Be referenced in AI answers |
| Metrics | Rankings, clicks, traffic | Citations, mentions, share of voice |
| User path | Click through to site | AI includes you in response |
| Platforms | Google, Bing | ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, AI Overviews |
| Optimization | Title tags, keywords, speed | Self-contained paragraphs, structured data, entity clarity |
| Credibility | Backlinks | Positive mentions across trusted platforms |

### 5 Principles for AI Visibility

1. **SEO fundamentals remain the core foundation** — Traditional SEO is still the base layer
2. **Entity clarity** — Clear, consistent brand identity everywhere
3. **Content extractability** — Self-contained, factual passages AI can quote
4. **Multi-platform presence** — Be everywhere AI looks (Reddit, YouTube, review sites, directories)
5. **Reputation signals** — Third-party mentions, reviews, citations

## Critical Rules

1. **Never skip QA verification** — every phase must pass Execute, Verify, Test, Document
2. **Never claim work is done without screenshot evidence**
